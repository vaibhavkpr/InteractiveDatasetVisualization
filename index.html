Vehicle Fuel Economy 1984 - 2017

About the dataset

The Vehicle Fuel Economy Estimates, 1984-2017 dataset on Kaggle offers a comprehensive look at the fuel efficiency of various vehicles sold in the United States from 1984 to 2017. The dataset was created and maintained by the EPA, which regulates fuel economy standards for vehicles in the country. It contains a wealth of information on each vehicle, including make, model, engine size, transmission type, fuel type, and estimated city and highway miles per gallon (MPG) ratings. Researchers, policymakers, and the public frequently use this dataset to analyze trends in fuel efficiency and develop policies aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions and improving fuel economy. It is also used by automakers and industry analysts to evaluate vehicle efficiency and compare the performance of different models. Overall, the Vehicle Fuel Economy Estimates dataset is a crucial resource for anyone interested in understanding the environmental impact of transportation and the factors that affect vehicle fuel efficiency.
